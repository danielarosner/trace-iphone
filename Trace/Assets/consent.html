You are being asked to volunteer in a research study. By using this application you are consenting to this participation.

PURPOSE

The purpose of this research is to investigate the ways in which you use mapping tools to move through public life. We are interested in understanding those experiences of movement (such as walking) and intimate communication in public life. We are also interested in exploring opportunities for emerging technologies or technologies that are not yet developed that may be useful for your purposes.

Participants of this study will be observed and participating with researchers, surveyed about their practice, interviewed about their relationship to their community, friends and family, and/or asked to use a set of tools for one or more hours.

You are being asked to participate because you are 18 years of age and a member of a community organization in Seattle, WA.

PROCEDURES

As a participant in this research, you are volunteering to take part using the Trace application for a length of time of your choosing.

RISKS

We do not expect our study to involve more than minimal risk; therefore, the possibility of unanticipated adverse events would be minimal. Possible risks or discomforts could arise from not being able to complete a task, learning a new set of interactive features, or standard risks associated with using a map to navigate a neighborhood. You should use override the application's routing instructions at any point along your path and use your best judgement while following the instructions. Negative outcomes pose a minimal risk to subjects and will be managed on the spot by the individual investigator and the TAT Lab staff assisting with the experiment.

BENEFITS

Your participation in this research will also aid in the design and development of technologies for navigation and communication.

SOURCE OF FUNDING

The study team and/or the University of Washington is receiving financial support from the National Science Foundation (NSF).

CONFIDENTIALITY

Several steps will be followed to keep your personal information confidential in this study. The data that is collected will be kept private to the extent allowed by law. To protect your privacy, records will be kept in locked files and only study staff will be allowed to view them. Your name will not appear when results of this study are presented or published.

To make sure that this research is being carried out in the proper way, the University of Washington IRB may review study records. The Office of Human Research Protections may also look at study records.

COSTS TO YOU

There are no costs to you to participate in this study other than your time and creative input.
